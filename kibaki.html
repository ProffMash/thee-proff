Rais Mwai Kibaki (in full Emilio Mwai Kibaki) was born into a family of eight children on November 15th 1931 in Gatuyaini Village,Othaya Division, Nyeri, in Kenya's Central Province.

He is the youngest son of the late Kibaki Githinji and the late Teresia Wanjiku.

The late Mwai Kibaki went to Gatuyaini Village School where he completed sub "A" and sub "B" which are the equivalent of standard one and two.He then joined Karima Mission School for three more classes of primary school.

In 1994, he moved to Mathari School(now called Nyeri High School) a boys' boarding school where he slept on a bed made from a wooden board and hay. Here he completed Standard four to six. At Mathari School he learned carpentry and masonry, as it was the duty of students to repair furniture and provide material for maintaining the school's buildings. Here, he also learned to grow his own food.

Between 1947 and 1950 he attended Mang'u High School,here he excelled with a maximum of six points in his "O" level examination. This acadmic perfomance earned him a scholarship to Makerere Univeristy College,Uganda-One of the top schools in Africa.

He moved to Makerere University to persue a Bachelor of Arts degree in Economics, History and Political Science. At the University, he was the Chairman of the Kenya Students Association and the Vice-Chairman of Makerere Students Guild

in 1995 he graduated as the best student in his class with a First Class Honours Degree (BA) in Economics, History and Political Science. After his graduation he workedas the Assistant Sales Manager for Shell Company of East Africa - Uganda.

Having graduated as the best student in his class he earned himself a scholarship to continue further studies in the United Kingdom. He enrolled at the London School of Economics and here he achieved M.Sc. with Distinction in Public Finance.

In 1963, Mwai Kibaki made his entry into elective politics by contesting the Donholm Constituency (which is now Madaraka) in Nairobi Province, and he won on a Kanu Ticket.

Hon. Mwai Kibaki was a keen golfer and was one of the longtime members of the Muthaiga Golf Club.

Kibaki was married to Lucy Muthoni from 1961 until her deathin 2016. They had four children: Judy Wanjiku, Jimmy Kibaki, David Kagai and Tony Githinji. They also had several grandchildren: Joy Jamie Marie, Rachel Muthoni, Mwai Junior, and Krystina Muthoni.

1. Free Primary Education
Kibaki introduced free primary education program and made it accessible to everyone, especially children from ages of six years to thirteen years. This program resulted in a record number of children attending school. Within his first term in office, one million chidren who previosly lacked the financial means to attend school enrolled in public primary schools.

2. Revitalization of Agriculture
Mwai Kibaki empowered farmers and expanded the farming acreage. He opened up failed irrigation schemes and the mechanisation of agriculture. These activities led to a boom in Agriculture in Kenya.
Additionally, Kibaki endorsed projects to rehabilitate and expand irrigation schemes such as Bura, Hola, West kano, Mwea, Nzoia, Ahero and Katilu. This increased area under irrigation from 119,200 heactares to 151,800 hectares. In addition, new schemes were opened up in the Yala swamp, Katilu and Lokubae in Turkana, Kibwezi, Masinga, Kiambere among others.
